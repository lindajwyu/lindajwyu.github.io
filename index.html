  • 2020 Some friends and I play this party game called "Elon Musk" where everyone at a party is handed a piece of paper with the same world problem on it (say, global poverty) except for one person who gets a slip of paper that just says "Elon Musk". Then everyone has to say exactly two words that contain a solution to the problem, but the person given "Elon Musk" doesn’t know what the problem and has to confidently come up with a solution to a pressing global problem despite not understanding exactly what it is. (Hence, why the game is called Elon Musk.)
    Due to COVID and the end of gatherings, most of our socialising has been over videoconferencing software which led me to create a prototype design of a website that would allow people to play this game no matter their physical distance (or lack of pen and paper).


    On the back end, a sever side script will generate a random number (n) between one and the total numbers of players inputted to assign as the "Elon" of the round. The player who is the n-th to query for a new round gets the topic "Elon Musk" while every other player gets a topic chosen from the topic pool that has not been selected by their group yet.
